Financial Advisors

What is a longevity benefit for financial portfolios? A longevity benefit is a capital markets solution designed to hedge late-life income risk in retirement portfolios. By allocating a small portion of a portfolio to a shared actuarial fund, advisors can generate structured cash payouts for clients at ages 80, 85, 90, and 95, driven by a yield that is uncorrelated to market performance.

Your questions, answered.

How does Savvly handle longevity risk without a life expectancy assumption?

The fund spreads longevity risk across all participants using actuarial math. No individual has to estimate how long they'll live. When members exit early or pass away, their uncollected growth stays in the fund and flows to those who remain. The fund's mechanics are driven by group life expectancy, not any single participant's guess.

How does Savvly affect my AUM?

It helps preserve it. A dedicated late-life income source means your client is less likely to liquidate growth positions during downturns. The primary portfolio stays invested longer. The Savvly allocation sits in a brokerage account that remains part of the advisory relationship.

What are the tax implications of in-kind payouts?

Savvly payouts can arrive as shares, not cash. There is no taxable event until the client sells. If the client holds and passes those shares to heirs, beneficiaries may receive a step-up in cost basis. Clients should consult their tax advisor for specifics.

Is this a deferred income annuity?

No. Savvly is an SEC-registered capital markets fund, not an insurance contract. Contributions are invested in S&P 500 ETFs held by third-party custodians. There is no insurance company involved.

Where are client assets held?

At US Bank. Savvly does not hold client funds on its own balance sheet. If Savvly were to cease operations, client assets remain at the custodian under the fund's governing documents.

Do I need an insurance license to offer Savvly?

No. Savvly is a capital markets product registered with the SEC.

Can I include Savvly in a model portfolio?

Yes. The allocation can be integrated into existing portfolio models as a dedicated longevity sleeve. Savvly provides advisor-facing materials and implementation support.

How does the Fair Exit Rule work?

The early withdrawal value is calculated as 75% of your contribution plus an additional 1% for each year held, capped at 100%. This percentage is applied to the lesser of your original investment (excluding any sales load) or its current market value and is calculated for each remaining scheduled payout. For a full breakdown of assumptions and legal disclosures, please review the fund prospectus.

Why doesn't the estate get back 100%?

The remaining portion is what funds the increased payouts for participants who reach 80 and beyond. That tradeoff is what makes the longevity benefit work, and why participants who live longer can receive substantially more than a traditional investment alone.

How do I get my firm to approve Savvly?

Savvly works with your compliance and investment committee to answer questions and complete due diligence. Most advisors can't add products unilaterally. We're built for that process.
