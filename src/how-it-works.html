How It Works

How does the Savvly longevity benefit work? Savvly is a capital markets solution designed to complement your existing 401(k) or IRA. By contributing a small monthly amount to a shared longevity pool invested in S&P 500 ETFs, you can unlock structured cash payouts at ages 80, 85, 90, and 95 to help replenish your primary portfolio.

Your 401(k) was built for 65
Savvly was built for 100

You don't need to replace your retirement plan. You just need to make sure it lasts

Savvly complements your existing 401(k) or IRA. It was designed for the years those accounts weren't built to cover, the stretch between your mid-seventies and the rest of your life, when traditional savings are most likely to run short.

Savvly spent four years earning its SEC registration. That was deliberate. Something this different needed to prove its credibility before asking anyone to participate.

Market returns + Biological hedge

We figured out how to make biology a hedge for your investments. Because the longevity pool is driven by life expectancy, not market performance, it's not affected by the forces that put your 401(k) at risk. Everyone's money goes into a shared risk pool, gets invested in the S&P 500, and the longer you live, the bigger your payout. People who die earlier get their money back, and the growth they would have earned stays in the pool for everyone else. It's SEC-registered, and it's not insurance.

How the Longevity Benefit Works

1. Small, automated monthly contributions

You contribute a set amount each month into your Savvly account. We recommend starting with $10 to $100. This runs parallel to your existing retirement accounts. You are not moving your savings. You are adding a layer alongside them.

2. Two sources of growth

Your contributions are invested in S&P 500 ETFs held by institutional custodians like US Bank. That is the first source of growth: market returns, the same way your current investments work.

The second source is the pool itself. When members exit the pool early, whether by choice or by death, their uncollected growth is redistributed to everyone who remains. The longer you stay in the pool, the more of that redistribution flows to you. This is the mechanism that makes longer life more valuable, not less.

3. Structured payouts at milestone ages

Savvly distributes your accumulated longevity benefit as cash payouts at four points: age 80, 85, 90, and 95. These are designed to arrive around the time traditional retirement savings are most likely to thin out, so they can replenish your primary portfolio when it matters most.

The payout schedule is structured as follows: 40% at age 80, 30% at age 85, 20% at age 90, and 10% at age 95.

What Happens If Your Plans Change

Life is unpredictable. Your money is never locked away.

If you withdraw early: You can close your Savvly account at any time. There is no lock-up period and no surrender penalty.

If you pass away before age 80: Your estate receives a return based on a straightforward formula: 75% of your initial investment, plus 1% for every year you were invested (up to 100%), applied to the lesser of your initial investment or the current market value of your account.

The uncollected growth from early exits and deaths is what funds the longevity benefit for everyone who remains in the pool. That is the mechanism. The people who stay longer benefit from the people who leave earlier. And the people who leave earlier or their families receive a defined, transparent return.

[+] How is the Savvly Longevity Benefit different from an annuity?

Savvly is structured as a capital markets investment fund, not an insurance contract. Contributions are invested in low-cost S&P 500 ETFs and held by third-party custodians. When pool members exit, their uncollected growth is redistributed directly to the remaining participants as a longevity bonus. There is no insurance company involved, no complex contract terms, and no corporate intermediary retaining the surplus.

[+] How is the Longevity Benefit taxed?

Savvly follows the tax treatment of the account type. If held in a qualified account (such as a traditional IRA or 401(k)), contributions and growth are taxed upon withdrawal under standard qualified account rules. If held in a non-qualified account, growth and payouts are generally subject to capital gains treatment. Consult your tax advisor for guidance specific to your situation.

[+] Where is my money held?

Savvly does not hold client funds. Your assets are held by regulated third-party custodians, currently US Bank, and invested in ETFs from providers like Vanguard and Fidelity. You maintain ownership of your funds at all times. In the unlikely event that Savvly ceases operations, your assets remain yours, held at the custodian.

[+] What happens if I leave my employer?

Your Savvly account is fully portable. It belongs to you, not your employer. If you change jobs, the account travels with you and continues to grow toward your milestone payouts.

[+] Is there a minimum contribution?

Savvly recommends monthly contributions starting at $10 to $100, though the specific minimum may vary depending on your employer's plan structure. Contact your benefits administrator or financial advisor for details.
