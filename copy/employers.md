**Block 4**

**What your employees actually get.**

Each participating employee contributes a small monthly amount, as little as \$10 to \$100, into the Savvly Longevity Pool. This runs alongside their existing 401(k) or IRA. They are not moving their savings. They are adding something new.

Their money is invested in S&P 500 ETFs held by institutional custodians like US Bank. That gives them market returns the same way their current accounts do.

But the pool adds a second layer. When members leave the pool early or pass away, their uncollected growth stays in the pool and flows to everyone who remains. The longer your employee stays in, the more that flow works in their favor.

Starting at age 80, the pool pays out in structured cash installments: 40% at 80, 30% at 85, 20% at 90, and 10% at 95. These arrive around the time traditional savings are most likely to run thin.

If an employee leaves the company, the account goes with them. It's fully portable. If they need to cash out early or if they pass away before 80, they or their estate receive 75% of their initial investment plus 1% for every year they participated. Their money is never trapped.

**\[+\] How is the Savvly Longevity Benefit different from an annuity?**

Savvly is a capital markets investment fund, not an insurance contract. Contributions are invested in low-cost S&P 500 ETFs held by third-party custodians. When pool members exit, their uncollected growth is redistributed directly to remaining participants. There is no insurance company involved and no complex contract terms.

**\[+\] Does offering Savvly create fiduciary risk for the employer?**

No. Savvly operates as an SEC-registered investment adviser. Assets are held by regulated third-party custodians. The employer provides access. Savvly manages the fund.

**\[+\] How is the Longevity Benefit taxed?**

Savvly follows the tax treatment of the account type. In a qualified account like a 401(k) or IRA, standard qualified account tax rules apply. In a non-qualified account, growth and payouts are generally subject to capital gains treatment. Employees should consult their tax advisor for specifics.

**\[+\] Is the benefit portable if an employee leaves?**

Yes. The account belongs to the employee. If they change jobs, it goes with them. They keep their progress toward their milestone payouts.
