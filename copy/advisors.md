What is a longevity benefit for financial portfolios? A longevity benefit is a capital markets solution designed to hedge late-life income risk in retirement portfolios. By allocating a small portion of a portfolio to a shared actuarial pool, advisors can generate structured cash payouts for clients at ages 80, 85, 90, and 95 from a source that is independent of market performance.

**Block 3**

**What happens inside the pool.**

Your client's contribution is invested in S&P 500 ETFs through institutional custodians. That gives them market returns, the same as any index allocation.

The pool adds a second source of return. When members exit the pool early or pass away, their uncollected growth stays in the pool and flows to everyone who remains. This reallocation is driven by life expectancy, not market performance. It operates independently of the forces that affect the rest of your client's portfolio.

Starting at age 80, the pool pays out in structured cash installments: 40% at 80, 30% at 85, 20% at 90, and 10% at 95. These arrive at the point in your client's life when traditional portfolio withdrawals do the most damage.

If your client needs to exit early or passes away before 80, they or their estate receive 75% of their initial investment plus 1% for every year they participated. The remaining growth stays in the pool.

**Block 6**

**\[+\] How does Savvly affect my AUM?**

It helps preserve it. By creating a dedicated cash source for late-life expenses, your client is less likely to liquidate growth equities during downturns. The primary portfolio stays invested longer. The Savvly allocation itself is held in a brokerage account that remains part of the advisory relationship.

**\[+\] Is this a deferred income annuity?**

No. Savvly is a capital markets fund, not an insurance contract. Contributions are invested in low-cost S&P 500 ETFs held by third-party custodians. When pool members exit, their uncollected growth flows directly to remaining participants. There is no insurance company involved.

**\[+\] How is the longevity benefit taxed?**

Savvly follows the tax treatment of the account type. In a qualified account, standard qualified account tax rules apply. In a non-qualified account, growth and payouts are generally subject to capital gains treatment. Clients should consult their tax advisor for specifics.

**\[+\] Where are client assets held?**

Assets are held by regulated third-party custodians, currently US Bank. Savvly does not hold client funds on its own balance sheet. If Savvly were to cease operations, client assets remain at the custodian, protected under the fund's governing documents.

**\[+\] Do I need an insurance license to offer Savvly?**

No. Savvly is a capital markets product. No insurance license is required.

**\[+\] Can I include Savvly in a model portfolio?**

Yes. The allocation can be integrated into your existing portfolio models as a dedicated longevity sleeve. Savvly provides advisor-facing materials and implementation support.
