## What you say when they ask what else is out there

Savvly is a shared risk pool where employees contribute a small monthly amount alongside their existing 401(k). The money is invested in S&P 500 ETFs held by institutional custodians like US Bank. It grows with the market.

But the pool adds something a regular investment account can't. When members leave the pool early or pass away, their uncollected growth stays in the pool and flows to everyone who remains. The longer someone stays in, the more that works in their favor.

Starting at age 80, the pool pays out structured cash: 40% at 80, 30% at 85, 20% at 90, and 10% at 95.

If someone leaves early or dies before 80, they or their estate receive 75% of their initial investment plus 1% for every year they participated.

No medical underwriting. No "use it or lose it." The employee owns the account and takes it with them if they change jobs.

## Your questions, answered

**\[+\] Do I need a securities license to offer Savvly?**

No. If you are a licensed benefits or insurance broker, you can introduce Savvly to your employer clients. Savvly operates as the SEC-registered investment adviser and handles the securities side. You make the referral.

**\[+\] How does the broker relationship work?**

You introduce Savvly to your employer clients. Savvly handles enrollment, fund management, compliance, and employee education. You maintain your client relationship and receive a referral fee. Details are in the Broker Partner Kit.

**\[+\] How is the longevity benefit different from an annuity?**

Savvly is a capital markets fund, not an insurance contract. Contributions are invested in low-cost S&P 500 ETFs held by third-party custodians. When pool members exit, their uncollected growth flows directly to remaining participants. There is no insurance company involved and no complex contract terms.

**\[+\] How hard is this to set up for an employer?**

Most employers are live in under a week. Savvly integrates with existing payroll and benefits platforms. There is no new infrastructure to build and no ERISA nondiscrimination testing required.

**\[+\] Is the benefit portable for employees?**

Yes. The account belongs to the employee. If they change jobs, it goes with them and continues to grow toward their milestone payouts.

**\[+\] What about employees who are skeptical or worried about risk?**

The Fair Exit Rule is the answer. If an employee needs to leave early or passes away before 80, they or their estate receive 75% of their initial investment plus 1% for every year they participated. Nobody is trapped. That makes it an easy conversation.
